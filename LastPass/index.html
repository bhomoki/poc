Advanced settings

Re-prompt for your LastPass master password before you:

Add another layer of protection by requiring a second login step. Keep the bad guys out, even if they steal your password through malicious software.

When you turn on multifactor authentication for your LastPass account, you can choose to 'trust' a device. When logging in on a trusted device, you will not be asked to provide your multifactor authentication. Trusted devices automatically expire after 30 days, after which you must re-trust them.

Control what smartphones and tablets may access your LastPass account. By default, a unique identifier (UUID) is created to track each device, but you can edit the device label at any time.

Need to turn off LastPass on specific websites? Add a website as a Never URL if you do not want LastPass to take action when you visit it.

If you have the same login credentials across multiple different websites, you can mark the websites as 'equivalent'. 'Global' domains are ones created by LastPass.

When you're logging in to a website, LastPass shows all login entries having a similar URL. If you want to see fewer entries, you can set a URL Rule to limit which logins are shown.
